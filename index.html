Plinky is an 8-voice polyphonic touch synthesiser that specialises in fragile, melancholic sounds.

It fits into 24HP eurorack cases, but can also be played as a standalone desktop mini-synth over CV or MIDI, with line-level stereo input and a headphone output, all powered by USB.

When is it available?

Plinky is fully open source. It is is available as a full DIY Kit at Thonk (UK), Exploding Shed (EU) and Nightlife Electronics (CAN).

Built Plinky are available at Schneidersladen (EU), Thonk (UK), Perfect Circuit (US) and Nightlife Electronics (CAN). Or you can make your own!

Plinky+ is the extra-large deluxe version of Plinky.

Plinky+ doubles the size of the play surface. It comes with a metal enclosure, a clear dustcover, a bigger display, symmetric 6.35mm audio outputs and a printed manual. It includes all the Eurorack I/O of Plinky and the Expander, and comes assembled or as a solderless DIY Kit.

Where can I get one?

Assembled Plinky+ are available at Thonk (UK), Perfect Circuit (US) and Nightlife Electronics (CAN).

It is also available as a full DIY Kit at Thonk (UK), Exploding Shed (EU) and Nightlife Electronics (CAN). It does not require soldering and contains all the parts you need to assemble the synth. You need to bring your own tools.

Plinky now welcomes 3 small 12HP x 1U modules to the family: Bib, Buzzrito and Blueberry.

These little buddies provide a plinky style stereo reverb, delay, and drive (Bib), a rich swarm oscillator (Buzzrito), and a generative touch sequencer (Blueberry). They take the touch-enabled sensitivity of Plinky, and break it out into 3 small (12HP) modules for your 1U eurorack row.

If you don't have a 1U eurorack row, there is an option to mount all 3 in a single 12HP standard Eurorack module.

They are the result of a collaboration between Plinky community members mmalex and CrazyEmperor893.

Available now!

The buddies are available now as kits from Thonk. Bib, Buzzrito, Blueberry and the 3U mega combo bundle.

Bib is available now, in prebuilt form from the Crey Emporium, with the other buddies to follow extremely soon.

You can read the manuals (Bib, Buzzrito, Blueberry) right now, and build guides (Bib, Buzzrito, Blueberry) in the Docs section of this site.

For sneak peaks and sound demos, follow PlinkySynth and CrazyEmperor893 on instagram.
